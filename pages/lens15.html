Why Half-Life

Back

Half life impacted the game industry in a way that no other developers could have imagined. A new feature they added to the game is storytelling without shoving it down the players throat. So instead of fighting the player by taking away game play with cinematics to tell the story or dialogue that couldn't be skipped half life told its story in a different way. The player would learn the story line by naturally progressing through the game from either talking to NPC characters like other games or unlike other games pick up little pieces from eavesdropping from NPC talking to one another. Like real life you can walk past a group of people and get a little of what they're talking about even if it wasn't your intention. So using this realistic feature Half Life was able to tell its story without forcing the player to learn it.

They would naturally grow and learn it as the game went on from the actions they made. Cinematics were now used more successfully because instead of being wasted on catching the player up in the story lounge it could be used for anything the developers wanted to help make the game more successful. Half life sold 9.3 million copies by the year 2008 and had made a big influence on the market we see today. A game made in more modern time was influenced by Half Life was the game Heartworm. This low poly 3D survival game took graphical inspirations from Half Life. So even still to this day Half life inspires other game makers in their craft.

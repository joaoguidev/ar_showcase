The storyline is precious and adds value to even the parts that in other games the player might feel the urge to skip it. The use of in game events instead of cutscenes keeps the immersion and adds to the suspense of the game as well as not taking the player's attention away. The initial scene, where the player is arriving at Black Mesa, had it been a cutscene the player would be more distant, it would be less personal therefore taking away from the value of it. Therefore been present, right there and then, adds value to the experience. It is very well implemented too. It is entertaining and constantly there are things to look at. Moving boxes or robots carrying their tasks while the player slides on the train.

As mentioned on the lens of time, compartmentalizing the game into chapters and small bits separated by events makes possible a step by step experience shortening the time between something happening therefore keeping the player focused and always stimulated. This in itself adds value to the experience and if a segment is not pleasant to the player, it is just one of several segments so the chances to bring back the player to a situation where he/she sees value again are greater and less far apart.

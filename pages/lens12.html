From the get go the player is presented with a sense of mystery and of being inserted into something important as you are transported around the Black Mesa facilities by a suspended train that moves you around. The sheer scale of the laboratory and the nature of the brief glances of what is being done there is enough to set the mood of the game to come and therefore it is an excellent beginning. The visual identity of the game , of course, given the hardware of the time, is remarkable and recognizable up until today and proof of that is that games and mods based on Half-Life were promptly recognized and had a very distinct and unique look and feel to it.

While underground the atmosphere is rich with scientists speaking sciency language, constant announcements through the speakers in rooms and hallways gives the corporate feeling regarding the huge Black Mesa corporation.. The sheer sense of the scale of the underground installation is incredible. The player really feels in a huge laboratory.

The atmosphere has to convey the science fiction aspect of it while exploring the idea of a government cover up of a major mishap and also the high tech corporative style of Black Mesa while mixing it with an alien invasion and succeeding in the mix. Given the fact that this game was the genesis to several very good mods sounds like health regeneration, the weird sound when you press ‘e’ on a not interactable object and the crowbar swing was broadly disseminated and it by itself constitute an element that even when encountered in a different game transports one’s mid to Half-Life. That’s how memorable the atmosphere of the game was.

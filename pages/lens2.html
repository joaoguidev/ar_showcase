Lenses of Target Audience & Gameplay Experience

The experience Half life gave a player in 2004 was like no other in its time. The game had an awesome sensation of audio. Anyone who has played the game or has even been on the internet since that time knows the sound of the classic crowbar that you use in the game as your main tool. There was just something about the sound of swinging that crowbar and smashing crates that audibly is pleasurable to the player. The game plays a lot through memory as the more you traverse the game the more knowledge you learn on what is happening around you. The more you go further in the game the more secrets you find out to help fully understand what is happening while the facility falls in chaos around you.

The challenges and puzzles in Half life are what really get a player thinking. Due to the facility falling apart or being raided by the US Army you have to figure a way to get across such difficult levels of terrain without dying just so you can get further into escaping. The last piece is the emotion you get from this game. All game all you can think is to escape and survive but the further and further you go into this game you figure out all the enemies that the US Army was sent to destroy are under mind control and need to be released so there isn't a massive war between civilization and the outer world. You get to feel really sorry so using that massive brain of yours you find a way to escape your own reality to go to a different world and defeat the one who has set all his people under mind slavery. Saving both the human and intergalactic species.

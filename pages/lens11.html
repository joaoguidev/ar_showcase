Half-Life is an excellent example of keeping the player's attention focused on the game by dividing it into chapters and within these chapters advancing the story by scripted events that happen as the player progresses on the map. This works as a cue for the player to inform that the plotline is advancing on that segment but provides a larger expectation and curiosity when it comes to going to the next chapter. This technique keeps the player focused on the task at hand, while with a larger goal in mind ( chapter change), helped by the interesting storyline, essentially vanishing the sense of time passing from the player's senses while providing constant segmented feedback that the player is advancing on the storyline.

This style of feeding the player with small bytes of information, punctuated by in-game scripts as opposed to cutscenes, grants a chance to extend the game length due to the immersion and the will of wanting to keep discovering the next bit of the storyline and see the next script and on a broader expectation , get to the next chapter. With that structure the game is on the right measure of gameplay time while still intriguing and interesting. The original Half Life even had 2 expansions and later a Half-Life 2.

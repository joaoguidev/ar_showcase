I’d say the memory of playing the games before is a bit of a problem in the half life games when it comes to immersion. There are a few times in the first half-life that become more common in the later games, where you are locked in a room with an npc who wants to have to explain the plot to you, which is nowhere near as immersive the second time ‘round. Unlike most modern games which fade into tightly choreographed cutscenes that make sure you see the scene in the best way possible the half-life games leave you in full control of your character, letting you misbehave and jump on npc’s heads as they try to patiently convey their exposition, never acknowledging the wacky things you are doing out of fear they are encouraging you. Of course this usually never happens on the first playthrough when you are still fully immersed, but once you get to the second and you already know everything they are going to say this situation becomes a lot more common. Not only does it destroy the tone the developers were intending, but it damages your ability to take the world anywhere near as seriously as the developers seem to want you to.

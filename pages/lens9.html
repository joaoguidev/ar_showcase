Lens of Freedom

Back

Half life gave the player the free choice of how they want to learn the story from the game. Like mentioned previously like it were a real job you could either talk to all your co-workers and find out what is happening or you can learn yourself from doing what you want. Even though in the end you were still following the same story the way you found out about it was the way the player wanted with their choices.

The game is made up from big open areas such as a minefield all the way to crawling through vents. So some places seemed a little concerning because you had to go where the game wanted you but in other areas like the minefield there were multiple ways and strategies to get through without blowing up.

The best thing was the speed of the challenges and puzzles that the player came across. Some of the players are in a peaceful area and have time to think what they want to try before they do it and other places they are rained down by hectic gun fire and must think in the moment. Either way the player can do what they deem is right to survive and beat the game. The game isn't too punishing if a player makes the wrong move because what makes it so good is all the ways you can complete a sector.

So when you mess up you just die and respawn right where you started to give it a go again. This helps the player learn and get better and make better decisions later on by adapting. The number of actions in the crucial areas is what matters the most when it comes to games of that era and half life did it perfectly.

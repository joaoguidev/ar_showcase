Mechanics: The game has you set sneaking through the Black mesa facility to try to get out safely. So the player had to interact with a lot of things. The source engine really helps make the game interactable. The whole mechanics were to break through boxes and move and shoot things so you can traverse through the facility. You would have to use the brilliant mind of the scientist Gordon freeman. Even though traversing the facility was a linear path you still had to interact with items like solving a puzzle to continue on.

Story: The storytelling is the reason why Half life is one of the most liked games out there for its time. Half life didn't tell its story line strictly through cut scenes like most games among the times. You as the player could overhear conversation from other scientists or talk to other scientists. So as if it were real life and you were walking to your job like Morgan freeman was you would learn from walking around and talking or not. Not only this but the story itself was spectacular and the way they got the story across was perfect. The story was about a secret government base where top researchers were brought to conduct experiments for the government but something goes wrong and you as the player must figure out how to fix things.

Aesthetics: What made half life look so aesthetically pleasing was the source engine that valve made. This engine changed the way games were made and played across the world. This engine not only gave more accessibility to the game developers but also the players. From the way it rendered objects to the freedom of design the user had with the engine was unlike anything at the time. Later on valve released the source engine to the public to play with and got lots of inspiration from the community for later games such as TF2.

Technology: The interaction with the AI really changed how a players game was laid out. While running through the Black Mesa facility the player will come across some police and scientists. When you interact with these chacterters they will have a few voice lines following their personal traits but that's the cool part. Like real life you can ask these people to either come and help you or follow you to stay still or mostly anything that could help your situation. So having a policeman with a gun would be handy because he could help you combat other enemies you might encounter but because the AI wasn't the smartest you didn't want it following you through a land mine field because he would most likely kill you and himself. It helped with the storyline because it made the game feel more realistic. While running through the facility you would find people and interact with them and they would all be the same for obvious reasons.

Half life perfectly used all four of these types when it came out. These four elements really help support another to overall lift the game and make it a gaming master piece.
